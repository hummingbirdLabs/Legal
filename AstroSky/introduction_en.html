Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions? We've Got Answers.

What is AstroSky?

AstroSky is a professional FITS image viewer for Windows 10 and 11 that turns raw astronomical survey data into stunning, wallpaper-quality images. It supports batch FITS import, one-click Auto Enhance, 12+ perceptually-uniform color maps, and exports up to 8K resolution — all 100% offline. Think of it as the easiest way to open FITS files and make them look beautiful.

Is AstroSky free?

Yes — AstroSky offers a free 30-day trial with all features fully unlocked. No credit card or account required. After the trial, a one-time purchase gives you lifetime access. There is no subscription — you buy it once, you own it forever.

Can I open FITS files on Windows without other software?

Absolutely. AstroSky is a dedicated FITS file viewer for Windows that opens .fits, .fit, and .fts files directly. Unlike generic image viewers, AstroSky understands FITS headers, WCS sky coordinates, and multi-extension data — it's the easiest way to view and convert FITS files on Windows.

Does AstroSky work offline?

Yes. After installation from the Microsoft Store, AstroSky works 100% offline. All image processing runs locally on your device — no internet connection is needed. Your FITS files never leave your computer.

What FITS file formats does AstroSky support?

AstroSky supports .fits, .fit, and .fts files, including multi-extension FITS (MEF) and data cubes (NAXIS=3) for spectral data visualization like radio astronomy cubes.

How do I export FITS images as wallpapers?

Select any image in the Gallery, click Export Image, and choose from resolution presets: 1080p, 1440p, 4K, 5K, or 8K. Pick PNG or JPEG format, optionally add a watermark with telescope details, and click Export. Your wallpaper-ready image is saved instantly.

What's the difference between Beauty Mode and Science Mode?

Beauty Mode (default) shows a clean, minimal interface with just Auto, Color Map, and Export — perfect for creating wallpapers. Science Mode expands full analysis tools: beam overlay, pixel readout, sky coordinates, PV diagrams, channel maps, moment maps, and image algebra. You can switch in under 100ms without reloading images.
